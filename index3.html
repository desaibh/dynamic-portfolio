Competitive Research

Strengths
  • What the company is doing well
  • Qualities that set the company apart from the competition
  • Human factors, such as talent of staff and team members
  • Assets, such as copyrights, trademarks, real estate, tools, and equiment
Weaknesses
  • What the company could improve
  • Difficulty stating a unique value proposition
  • Limitations based on existing resources
Opportunities
  • Goals the company could achieve with the right investment of resources
  • Uncovering trends to serve growing markets
  • Creating opportunities to serve underserved markets
  • Increasing brand recognition and brand loyalty with the right growth strategies
Threats
  • Road blocks that need to be overcome to achieve goals
  • Keeping an eye on competitors who are emerging in the same space
  • Tracking new regulations that impact the way companies do business
  • Assessing customer sentiments toward the brand

Metrics

SMART Goals / KPIs

Ideally speaking... objective[s] should be:

  • Specific – target a specific area for improvement.
  • Measurable – quantify or at least suggest an indicator of progress.
  • Assignable – specify who will do it.
  • Realistic – state what results can realistically be achieved, given available resources.
  • Time-related – specify when the result(s) can be achieved.

-- George T. Doran
